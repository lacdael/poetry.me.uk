My name is Christian Lacdael, and for my day job I am a programmer. I write poems because to keep my left brain active. It is my strong belief that the root cause of many of the ills of the modern world is a right brain domanance. While I mainly depend on my right brain for my day job, it is the left brain that finds solutions that have the right brain stumped.

I believe that change can be organically manifested via the spoken or written word. In the modern world where spoken and written word is so easily curtailed by gate keepers, I am deeply concern, but as I have no answer or power to address this, I can only press on staying true to my beliefs. By reading or hearing about the beauty and positive aspects of a subject, that subject will be perceived in a positive way, which in turn will better how it is treated; therefore manifesting a positive change.

As a child I enjoyed art, and so I was exposed to William Blake. The composition of William Blake's art has hints of spiritual geometry, which I feel is what its appeal to me was, as I am one who believes that only geomentry is fundemental, which everything else contructed. As a teenager I had a growing interest in folklore. I loved the poetry and playfulness in the superstitions, and beliefs. Often lore and beliefs say that which cannot be so concisely put into words. By taking the path of poetry, you gain a poet perspective of the world, relationships and literature. Poetry trains you to first read between the lines.
